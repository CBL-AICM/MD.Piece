# Recognizing Asthma: Wheezing, Night Cough, Exercise Triggers

## In one sentence

Asthma's **four classic symptoms** are wheezing, cough, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. But **"cough-variant asthma"** shows only cough — and is most often misdiagnosed as "lingering cough."

## Want to know more

### Typical presentation
- **Wheezing** (whistling sound on exhale)
- **Cough** (especially at night and early morning)
- **Chest tightness**
- **Shortness of breath**

### Triggers (learn to spot yours)
- **Allergens**: dust mites, pet dander, mold, pollen
- **Viral infections** (most common)
- **Cold air**
- **Exercise** (exercise-induced)
- **Occupational exposure**
- **Chemical irritants** (perfume, cleaning agents)
- **NSAIDs** (in sensitive individuals)

### Cough-variant asthma
- Cough is the primary or only symptom
- Lingering cough (> 3 weeks)
- Mostly at night
- Responds well to bronchodilators
- Easily misdiagnosed as chronic cough

### Acute attack severity

**Mild**
- Occasional shortness of breath, can speak full sentences
- 1–2 puffs of rescue inhaler resolves it

**Moderate**
- Speaks only in fragments
- Rescue inhaler effect is short
- Heart rate 100–120

**Severe**
- Only speaks single words
- Cannot lie flat
- Heart rate > 120
- SpO2 90–94%

**Life-threatening**
- **Cyanotic (blue) lips**
- Altered consciousness
- Silent chest (no audible wheeze = severe constriction)
- Slow heart rate (exhaustion)
- → **Call 119 immediately**

### Asthma in children
- Recurrent night cough
- Cough after exercise
- Cough lasting > 3 weeks after a cold
- Wheezing
- Clutching the chest

### Self-monitoring

**PEF (peak expiratory flow)**
- Home peak-flow meter
- Below 80% of personal best → warning
- Below 60% of personal best → acute attack

**Symptom diary**
- Rescue inhaler uses
- Number of nighttime coughs
- Activity limitation

### Signs of poor control
- Rescue inhaler ≥ 2 times/week
- Nighttime cough ≥ 2 times/month
- Frequent shortness of breath after exercise
- Acute attack ≥ 1/year

## When to see a doctor
- Recurrent wheezing or night cough
- Lingering cough > 3 weeks
- Poor response to rescue inhaler
- Signs of poor control
- Severe attack → emergency department

> Asthma symptoms come in **many shapes** — learning to recognize your own enables earlier treatment and better control.
