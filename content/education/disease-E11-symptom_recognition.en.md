# Recognizing Type 2 Diabetes: Often Silent, But There Are Clues

## In one sentence

Early type 2 diabetes is **almost symptom-free** — most people only find out through a **blood screening**. By the time you notice thirst and frequent urination, the disease is already mid-to-late stage.

## Want to know more

### Early warning signs (easily overlooked)
- Easily tired
- Wounds heal more slowly
- Vision fluctuates
- Frequent infections (yeast, urinary tract)
- Acanthosis nigricans (dark patches on neck or armpits)
- Unexplained weight loss (yes, **paradoxically** — insulin secretion has declined)

### Mid-stage classic "three highs and one low"
- Polyuria (more night urination)
- Polydipsia (thirst even after drinking)
- Polyphagia (hungry quickly)
- Weight loss

### Acute hyperglycemia (HHS — hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state)
- Severe dehydration
- Altered consciousness
- Mostly in older adults
- High mortality
- Different from DKA (no ketoacidosis)

### Hypoglycemia (after medication)
- Trembling, palpitations, cold sweat
- Hunger, dizziness
- Oral antidiabetic drugs (especially sulfonylureas) can also cause it

### When to get screened
- Age ≥ 45 (every 3 years)
- Before 45 if any of the following:
  - BMI ≥ 24
  - Family history of diabetes
  - History of gestational diabetes
  - Hypertension or dyslipidemia
  - Polycystic ovary syndrome

### Screening tools
- Fasting blood glucose
- HbA1c
- 75 g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT)

## When to see a doctor
- Any of the warning signs above
- Elevated fasting glucose on a routine check-up
- HbA1c in the "prediabetes" range (5.7–6.4%)
- Wanting a full screening

> Type 2 diabetes is **mostly found through screening** — don't wait until you "feel sick" to get tested.
